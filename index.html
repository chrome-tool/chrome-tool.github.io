Image Picker

Date: March 25, 2026

A lightweight Chrome extension that collects, previews, and downloads all images on any webpage — without leaving the tab. Features Comprehensive image collection — captures <img> tags, srcset candidates, <picture><source>, CSS background images, <video> posters, og:image meta tags, and JSON-LD structured data Modern format support — fully handles AVIF, WebP, and CDN-style URLs with ?format=avif query parameters Live badge — the floating launcher shows a real-time count of images found on the page, updating automatically as the page loads more content Floating launcher — drag to reposition, snaps to the left or right edge of the screen; badge moves to the inner side for visibility Smart panel placement — panel opens to the side of the launcher and flips above when the launcher is near the bottom of the screen Search & filter — filter images by filename, alt text, or host; set a minimum size to hide small icons and thumbnails Sort options — sort by largest, smallest, widest, or tallest Per-image actions — open in new tab, copy URL, or download each image individually Batch actions — select all visible images, copy all URLs, or batch download in one click Click outside to close — click anywhere outside the panel to dismiss it; Esc key also works Usage Navigate to any webpage The floating launcher button appears at the bottom-right corner — the number on it shows how many images were found Click the launcher to open the image panel Browse, search, filter, and sort the collected images Use Open, Copy, or Download on individual images, or select multiple and use the batch action bar Drag the launcher to reposition it; it snaps to the left or right edge Click outside the panel or press Esc to close Reviews   5 out of 5 stars
